The AI Yearbook trend on favorite stars from Mean Girls actor to Ru Paul's Drag Race

Celebrities including Keke Palmer and Katharine McPhee have tried out the so-called 'AI Yearbook' trend, in which photos resembling a yearbook photoshoot are digitally generated

By
Joel LeaverShowbiz Reporter
  • 20:45, 7 Oct 2023
  • |
  • comments

A number of celebrities have tried out the so-called 'AI Yearbook' trend in which photos of them resembling those from a school yearbook in the 1990s are digitally generated.

It's proved popular on social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok recently. It's understood that some people have created them through the app Epik, which requires an in-app purchase, reportedly from $5.99 for the new trend.

The trend - which has attracted some criticism in light of artificial intelligence (AI) being an area of concern amid strikes in the entertainment industry - has been promoted through posts by various celebrities and content creators.

Among those who have taken part is actor and singer Keke Palmer, 30. She took to Instagram earlier this week to share 'yearbook photos' of herself. She wrote alongside them in the post: "Idk y'all ... I feel like mine ain't me fr #AIYearbook."

Keke - whose acting credits include Scream Queens and the film Nope (2022) - subsequently shared other photos using the filter. She wrote in the second post: "Why the man me look more correct - I'm hollering omg haha yassssss."

Jonathan Bennett, 42 - best known for his role as Aaron Samuels in Mean Girls (2004) - teased over the trend on social media too. He shared a series of photos with fans in the same week as 'Mean Girls Day,' which falls on October 3.

The photos that he shared with fans on Instagram included one that showed the actor sporting a shirt, waistcoat, a tie and a pair of glasses. He wrote: "I wish my school photos looked this good #90syearbook Thanks @facetune."

Others who've tried the trend include Katharine McPhee, 39. The actor - known for shows like Smash - shared photos on Instagram that showed her in an apparent yearbook photoshoot. One showed her in a double denim outfit.

The American Idol finalist teased that it didn't resemble her experience in the accompanying caption. She wrote in a message to her followers in the post on the platform: "Sad to say this is *NOT* what I looked like in high school."

Michelle Visage, 55 - who is known as a judge on RuPaul's Drag Race (RPDR) and some of its international versions - also took on the trend recently. She shared various photos with her followers on Instagram after trying out the trend.

She wrote alongside her photos: "First of all ... These are toxic cuz I would've OWNED my high school years if I served ALL OF THIS MUGGGGG. Second of all, it's giving me [drag artist Adore Delano] and [actor Trace Lysette] had a baby."

Adore, 34 - who is best known for having been a contestant on RPDR in its sixth season, before returning for the second season of RuPaul's Drag Race: All Stars - did actually try out the trend for herself, sharing the photos recently.

The singer and drag artist referenced the film Jawbreaker (1999) in the caption that accompanied her photos. Quoting a line from the film, she wrote in the post: "This is high school, Detective Cruz. What is a friend, anyway?"

Gymnast and content creator Olivia Dunne, 21 - who boasts almost 8 million followers on her TikTok account alone - shared her attempt with her followers on the platform recently. She uploaded a video that showed her photos.

Olivia wrote in the caption that she didn't get to have any photos taken for a yearbook towards the end of high school. She explained to her followers: "Never took senior pics 'cause I was homeschooled… so here ya go #aiyearbook."

Hoda Kotb, 59, discussed the trend on the Today Show recently alongside her co-host Jenna Bush Hager. The segment of the NBC show, which aired earlier this week, saw the broadcasters' yearbook photos unveiled to viewers.

Reacting to one of her photos, which showed her sporting a denim shirt, Hoda commented: "Okay, that was never the case." She subsequently responded to other photos in the trend, including dismissing that she had "feathered hair."

Among the other celebrities who have posted about the trend has been content creator James Charles, 24. He shared a series of photos of himself from the trend with his followers on both Instagram and TikTok earlier this week.

The makeup artist's photos included one that showed him wearing a blue shirt, a tie, and a pair of glasses whilst holding a book. Alongside the photos, James wrote in the accompanying caption on both platforms: "I'm scared."
